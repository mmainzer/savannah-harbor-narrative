Imagine that each of the circles below represent 7,000 shipping containers. The Port of Savannah has to process, inspect, and move thousands of these every day.

The Port's capacity has steadily expanded over the past decade. In 2006, the Port was processing a little over 2 million individual containers.

Activity stalled during the recession's first year, but by 2012, the Port had processed nearly 3 million TEUs in that year.

Activity has picked up more rapidly at the Port since the recession. In 2018, a new record was hit, with over 4.3 million TEUs coming through the port.

With a strong economy expected to continue, the Port is projected to handle over 5 million TEUs in 2019.
